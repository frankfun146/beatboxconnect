The Kick Drum is one of the most fundamental sounds in beatboxing, along with the hi-hat and snare. These three sounds form the basic foundation of beatboxing.

To learn the kick drum, try saying a word that starts with the letter 'b', like 'bob' or 'bus'. Instead of vocalizing the word, focus on the pressure in your mouth. Don’t actually say the word out loud; instead, concentrate on the pressure you create.

Pro Tip: It’s all about the mouth pressure. You want to feel the force without vocalizing the word itself!

Liproll is one of my favourite beatboxing sounds! It is a very versatile and powerful sound especially on mic. And it is actually not that hard to learn. I remember it took me 1 week to actually produce the sounds. At first of course if sounds terrible, but as you pratice more, you will notice that your lips start to soften, and eventually you will be able to produce the liproll. So it is all about practice!

To learn the Basic liproll, first drop your jaw. Then tilt your jaw to the left/right. Finally just try to make a kissing sound on the oppisite side of where you tilt your jaw. For me i use my right side to make the liproll, so i tilt my jaw to the left.

Pro Tip: In essence, learning liproll is kind of like the process of softening your lips. Once your lips are soft by practive, you will be able to produce the lip roll! So if you have not got it, do not worry! It takes time and efforts. Just follow the right instructions and keep doing it, you will get it one day!
